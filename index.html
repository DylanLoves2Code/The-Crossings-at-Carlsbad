HDCP 7 Tiered Green
Tee
Hit Driver (250 yds). Aim center-right — there is plenty of room on the right side. Do NOT cut the corner left (bunker is your target marker, not a line to carry). This is target golf — put it in play.
Remain
~166 yds out. Hit 7 Iron (155 yds). The coastal downhill lie may play shorter — club up if pin is past the middle ridge. Green has a ridge across center: use extra club when pin is on the far tier.
Avoid
Left bunker off the tee. Approach bunkers flanking the green. Short-siding yourself on the wrong tier adds a full extra putt.

HDCP 3 Elevated Green
Tee
Hardest par 4 on the front. Driver (250 yds) but controlled. The canyon drops off — pay attention to the yardage to the canyon in front of you. Don't be hero from an awkward lie.
Remain
~130 yds uphill. Hit 8 Iron (145 yds) — the uphill lie to the green means it will play like 145+. The approach tends to play a club longer because of the elevation gain. Coastal breeze headwind adds another half club.
Avoid
Canyon short of the green — the #1 scoring hazard here. When in doubt, take MORE club. Drop area is behind back-left of green.

PAR
3
195 yds (Blue)
HDCP 13 Split / Hidden Green
Tee
A tricky par 3. Use 4 Hybrid (210 yds) — the green is hidden behind a large mound in the front center. The green is split into two sides; check the pin location board on the tee box carefully.
Wind
The prevailing coastal breeze can push the ball right. Factor in a half-club for wind and aim slightly left of the flag if it's on the right side.
Avoid
The mound short of the green — it can funnel miss-hits sideways. Go after the correct side of the green; a front-left pin is not the same as a back-right pin here.

Tee
Driver (250 yds). Reachable in 3 if you play smart. There are pot bunkers dividing the fairway — the left side is shorter and tighter; the right side gives a better angle to the green. Take the right side.
2nd
~317 yds remain. Lay up with 4 Hybrid (210 yds) to ~107 yds. Avoid the pot bunkers in the center of the fairway at your second shot distance.
3rd
~107 yds. Gap Wedge (100 yds) or Sand Wedge — uphill and into coastal breeze may require an extra club. Aim center, avoid greenside bunkers.

HDCP 1 Tiered Green
Tee
The #1 handicap hole — toughest on the course. Driver (250 yds). The target bunker is your aim point; don't cut the corner. Be aware of the prevailing breeze pushing left-to-right.
Remain
~180 yds. This is between your 4-Hybrid (210) and 7-iron (155) — hit a controlled smooth 4 Hybrid with a three-quarter swing (~175 yds). Check the pin location: shot to the correct tier is critical here. Uphill and into coastal breeze adds 1–2 clubs.
Avoid
Wrong tier approach — being on the wrong level adds 3 putts fast. Don't leave it short; wind plays tricks here.

Short par 4 — forget about being a big hitter. Club selection is everything. Use 4 Hybrid (210 yds) or even less — leaves only ~79 yds in for a wedge.

PAR
3
113 yds (Blue)
HDCP 17 Narrow / Slopes Away
Tee
Short par 3. Use Pitching Wedge (125 yds) with a three-quarter swing. Check the wind and pin location carefully — as the green goes to the back it slopes away slightly and narrows.
Green
Back-pin adds complexity — the green narrows and tilts away, so a ball going too far easily runs off. Aim center/front on back pin positions.
Avoid
Overclubbing runs you off the back of a narrowing green. Sand bunkers flank the green. Take dead aim at the center and let your wedge spin hold the green.

The easiest hole on the course. Pitching Wedge (125 yds) — a full PW right at the pin. Check the wind first; coastal breeze can affect even short par 3s.

HDCP 14 Elevated / Trees
Tee
Longer par 3. Plays between your 7 iron (155) and 4 hybrid (210). Hit a controlled 4 Hybrid with an easy swing (~165-170 yds). Wind direction is critical here.
Green
Green is elevated and framed by trees and bunkers. Miss left or short rather than right (trees) or long (run-off). Aim center-left.
Avoid
Greenside bunkers (right and back). Short is recoverable; buried in a bunker right is not. When in doubt, take an extra half-club.

HDCP 2 Severely Undulating
Tee
The toughest finishing hole. Bunkers left, canyon right off the tee. Hit Driver but aim center — the coastal breeze makes this tee shot one of the most intimidating on the course. Do NOT chase the left bunker.
Remain
~131 yds. 9 Iron (135 yds). The green is severely undulating and framed by trees and bunkers — aim the middle of the green and accept a par. The green's slope creates brutal 3-putt potential.
Avoid
Canyon right off the tee (penalty), bunkers left of tee. Short-siding yourself near the greenside trees. Play this hole like a bogey is a win and a par is a victory lap.
